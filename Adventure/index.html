    Walk into the canyon
    September 2, 1999

    On the Second day, we hiked down into the inside of the canyon. It is way to hot and we underestimated the amount of water we would need. We walked around in the canyon for a while, but we ran out of water about 1/3 of the way back. When we finally got back we refilled all our water bottle and drank away. We were exhausted from walking around all day in the head and rested before we left the shade.

    Visited an Archeology center
    September 3, 1999

    We went back down into the canyon the next day, we brought a lot more water, which was actually quite heavy. Who knew water could be so heavy. We hiked back down to the inside of the canyon, luckly the archeology center was right at the bottom. There they showed us some of the bones and fossiles they have found still in the rock. We probally brought to much water and it was hard to hike back up.

    TERMS
    <Absolute Dating>
    A variety of methods for determining an actual date for an archeological site, such as radiocarbon dating or historical documents.

    <Archeology>
    The scientific study of past people through their sites and artifacts.

    <Disturbance>
    An event or sequence of events, either natural or cultural, that dislodges and disrupts archeological deposits.
